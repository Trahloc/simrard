# Genetics

The details of [norns](https://creatures.wiki/Norn), [grendels](https://creatures.wiki/Grendel), [ettins](https://creatures.wiki/Ettin) and [geats](https://creatures.wiki/Geat) in games in the [Creatures series](https://creatures.wiki/Creatures_series) - such as their [brains](https://creatures.wiki/Brain), [biochemistry](https://creatures.wiki/Biochemistry) and appearance - are to a great degree dictated by virtual genetics using a simulated [genome](https://creatures.wiki/Genome); often called [Digital DNA](https://creatures.wiki/Digital_DNA).

Things which are defined in [genomes](https://creatures.wiki/Genome) include:

- [brain](https://creatures.wiki/Brain) information ([lobes](https://creatures.wiki/Lobe), and the [dendrites](https://creatures.wiki/Dendrite) and [neurons](https://creatures.wiki/Neuron) they contain)
- [chemical](https://creatures.wiki/Chemical) reactions and [half-lives](https://creatures.wiki/Half-lives) (norns can become [immortal](https://creatures.wiki/Immortal) and such things when these [mutate](https://creatures.wiki/Mutation))
- [concentrations](https://creatures.wiki/Initial_concentration) of chemicals in the bloodstream at birth
- [biochemistry](https://creatures.wiki/Biochemistry) workings ([receptors](https://creatures.wiki/Receptor), [emitters](https://creatures.wiki/Emitter), and '[neuro emitters](https://creatures.wiki/Neuro_emitter)' which link with the brain systems)
- creature [appearance](https://creatures.wiki/Appearance); [pose](https://creatures.wiki/Pose), [pigments](https://creatures.wiki/Pigment) and [gait](https://creatures.wiki/Gait), among other things

Each individual '[gene](https://creatures.wiki/Gene)' of a genome can [mutate](https://creatures.wiki/Mutation) in various ways and lead to interesting new behaviours which the original designers never thought of.

There are [editors](https://creatures.wiki/Category:Genetic_Editors) available which let you manipulate the genetics directly and birth new creatures with them, such as the [Genetics Kit](https://creatures.wiki/Genetics_Kit).

Genetics are described for each breed, [genetic breed](https://creatures.wiki/Genetic_breed) and individual creature in [.gen](https://creatures.wiki/.gen) files. Documentation for each individual gene in a .gen file is collected in a [.gno file](https://creatures.wiki/.gno_file).

As of [Creatures 2](https://creatures.wiki/Creatures_2), creatures had around 800 genes, half of which were used for behavioural patterns and physical appearance, while the other half determined the [biochemistry](https://creatures.wiki/Biochemistry) and the nervous system.[[1]](https://creatures.wiki/Genetics#cite_note-1)

**Did You Know?** [Steve Grand](https://creatures.wiki/Steve_Grand) completely rewrote the code of *Creatures* when he included genetics![[2]](https://creatures.wiki/Genetics#cite_note-2)
